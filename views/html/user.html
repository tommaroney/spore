Forage verb

/fôrij,ˈfärij/
(of a person or animal) search widely for food or provisions.

Do not consume any mushroom unless you're 100 percent certain of what you’re picking.

Do not rely on only one source of information for identification.

Do not pull the mushrooms up by the roots; cut them.

Do not take more than you plan to eat.

Do not pick mushrooms that appear young or small; let them grow and spread spores to populate for future seasons.

Do not assume your body will react as other's do. Ease your way into consuming wild edibles.

Do be 100 percent certain of what you’re picking.

Do take pictures of mushrooms that you cannot identify to ask others and to gain knowledge.

Do make sure to enjoy being in the forest even if you do not find any mushrooms.

Do keep track of where you wonder to avoid getting lost. Let someone know where you're going and for how long.

Do follow all of these guidelines every time you forage for wild edibles.
